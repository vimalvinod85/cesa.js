Rules of Engagement (ROE)

Purpose: Deliver measurable outcomes that drive renewals, retention, and growth[cite: 1543].

Support vs. PS

  • Support: Broken items/incidents[cite: 1546].
  • PS: New builds or project work[cite: 1547].

Elevate vs. SA (CESA)

  • Elevate: Training/knowledge gaps[cite: 1548].
  • SA: Outcome mapping & value[cite: 1549].

Role Clarity: Orchestrator vs. Accelerator

CEE (Orchestrator) SA (Outcome Accelerator)
  • Owns value realization strategy[cite: 1551].
  • Defines Customer PoV & metrics[cite: 1553].
  • Identifies risk/expansion signals[cite: 1554].
  • Translates technical to business outcomes[cite: 1557].
  • Diagnoses adoption/workflow gaps[cite: 1558].
  • Validates expansion feasibility[cite: 1559].

Engagement Model: 4 Steps to Value

Step 1: Define

CEE defines business objectives and risk[cite: 1573, 1574].

Step 2: Align

Set clear roles and expected deliverables[cite: 1579, 1580].

Step 3: Interact

CEE frames business; SA leads technical translation[cite: 1585, 1586].

Step 4: Drive

SA delivers; CEE updates Success Plan[cite: 1589, 1590].

CEE Submission Checklist

Required context before engaging SA support[cite: 1506]:

  • Renewal date & ARR [cite: 1530]
  • Risk status (Green/Yellow/Red) [cite: 1531]
  • Clear Customer PoV & 3 outcomes [cite: 1507]
  • 2-3 sentence problem hypothesis [cite: 1533]
  • Desired measurable outcome [cite: 1534]

Note: If you cannot articulate the business objective, do not engage SA yet[cite: 1508].
